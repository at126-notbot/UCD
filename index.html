Rising sea levels are leading to increases in coastal flooding in Ireland. Extreme sea levels are also caused by a combination of waves, tides, and storm surges which Ireland is highly exposed to due to its location. Low-pressure systems cause elevated water height, with each millibar drop in air pressure causing a 1cm rise in the water column. Increased wind speeds cause increased wave heights, extreme wave heights have increased by 1cm per year between 1985 and 2018 globally, however under some scenarios extreme waves may become less common by 2100. When low-pressure systems combine with onshore winds dangerous storm surges can occur. If the storm surge occurs at the same time as a high spring tide then the coastal water level can be much higher than normal.
